## 🏋️‍♀️ Sample Specialization Program & Exercise Notes

*Note: This is a sample plan for training side delts four times a week. Perform these exercises FIRST in your workout, before you train other muscles.*

### Monday
*   **Lateral Raises**
    *   *Week 1:* 3 Sets, 10-15 Reps (3 Reps in Reserve)
    *   *Week 5 (Pre-deload):* Up to 6 Sets, 10-15 Reps (0 Reps in Reserve)
*   **Barbell Upright Rows**
    *   *Week 1:* 2 Sets
    *   *Week 5:* Up to 4 Sets
    *   *Notes:* Doing upright rows *after* lateral raises means your side delts are pre-exhausted. This ensures the side delts, not other muscles, are the limiting factor, creating a better mind-muscle connection.

### Wednesday
*   **Super ROM Laterals**
    *   *Week 1:* 2 Sets, 10-15 Reps
    *   *Week 5:* Up to 6 Sets, 10-15 Reps
*   **Cable Upright Rows**
    *   *Week 1:* 2 Sets
    *   *Week 5:* Up to 4 Sets

### Friday
*   **Cable Y Raises**
    *   *Week 1:* 2 Sets, 10-15 Reps
    *   *Week 5:* Up to 6 Sets
    *   *Notes:* You can face into the machine or away from it. Facing into the machine and pulling away targets the side delts beautifully but also heavily engages the traps. Facing away from the machine hits the traps a bit less if you want pure side delt isolation.

### Saturday
*   **Cable Y Raises (Myo-rep style)**
    *   *Week 1:* 2 Sets, 15-25 Reps
    *   *Week 5:* Up to 4 Sets
    *   *Notes:* Higher reps here provide a nasty burn. Volume doesn't peak as high on Saturday (only 4 sets compared to Friday's 6) to allow for systemic fatigue reduction before starting the heavy Monday session again.

## 🧠 General Side Delt Wisdom

*   **Why Specialize:** Specialization is needed if you want your side delts to grow disproportionately larger (to get that "cannonball" look) or if they are lagging behind the rest of your physique. It involves doing more for the side delts, which usually requires doing *less* for other muscle groups to manage systemic fatigue.
*   **Put Them First:** If you want your side delts to grow, you must front-burner them. Train them first or very early in your workout while you are fresh, rather than tacking a single lateral raise exercise at the end of a heavy chest or shoulder day.
*   **High Frequency is Key:** Side delts are mechanically positioned in a way that makes them somewhat impervious to extreme muscle damage. Because they recover incredibly quickly, you can—and often should—train them 3 to 4 times a week for maximum growth. 
*   **Find Your Rep Range:** Do not lift heavy just for the sake of lifting heavy if it hurts your joints and provides no mind-muscle connection. If sets of 20-25 reps with lighter weights give you the best pump, soreness, and growth without joint pain, that is the exact rep range you should use for your specialization block. 
*   **Progression and Duration:** Add sets from week to week if you are fully recovering days before your next session. To see true results, run a specialization phase for about 3 consecutive mesocycles (roughly 12-18 weeks total), taking active rest phases in between blocks.
