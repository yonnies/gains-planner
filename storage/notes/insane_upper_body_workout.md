* **T-Bar Row** - [00:05:04](https://youtu.be/OoA7a2lRxz8?t=304)[cite: 1]
    * *Notes:* Pull with your elbows, keeping them nice and tucked, rather than yanking the weight up[cite: 1]. Bring your chest up as the weight comes up to retract and get a significant contraction[cite: 1]. Keeping your chest up and chin tucked at all times helps avoid strain in your lower back[cite: 1].
* **Arnold Press** - [00:06:17](https://youtu.be/OoA7a2lRxz8?t=377)[cite: 1]
    * *Notes:* Unlike a standard military press, the Arnold press involves rotating the weight[cite: 1]. Extend fully at the top and rotate the weights back toward you so they are by your eyesight at the bottom[cite: 1].
* **Incline Bench Press (Dumbbell)** - [00:11:12](https://youtu.be/OoA7a2lRxz8?t=672)[cite: 1]
    * *Notes:* Keep your shoulders retracted back to isolate the chest[cite: 1]. This will naturally bring your chest up and create a slight arch in your back[cite: 1]. Drive upwards and use the negative rep to deeply target the chest area[cite: 1]. Your legs only serve to push the ground away for stability, not to lift the weight[cite: 1].

## 🧠 General Upper Body Wisdom[cite: 1]

* **Combine Push and Pull:** Combining pulling (back/biceps) and pushing (chest/shoulders/triceps) movements in one workout allows for hitting target muscles more frequently (twice a week instead of once) and can feel more efficient ([00:09:43](https://youtu.be/OoA7a2lRxz8?t=583))[cite: 1].
* **Use Structure to Dictate Weight:** A structured program gives you rep targets[cite: 1]. If an exercise calls for 6 reps and you can easily do 10, the weight is too light[cite: 1]. Your working weight should make the target rep range feel appropriately challenging ([00:03:29](https://youtu.be/OoA7a2lRxz8?t=209))[cite: 1].
* **The "Chest Up" Rule for Pulling:** A key tip for the entire workout: whenever you are rowing or pulling, bring your chest up[cite: 1]. This completely isolates the back muscles and allows for a deeper, stronger contraction while protecting the lower back ([00:16:03](https://youtu.be/OoA7a2lRxz8?t=963))[cite: 1].
* **Push Through the Final Set:** When you get to the last set and feel exhausted, dig deep[cite: 1]. This is your personal time to prove to yourself that you can do it—fight for that last rep because it counts the most ([00:13:05](https://youtu.be/OoA7a2lRxz8?t=785))[cite: 1].
