### Part 1: Heavy Compound Movements

* **T-Bar Row** - [00:05:04](https://youtu.be/OoA7a2lRxz8?t=304)
    * *Notes:* Pull with your elbows, keeping them nice and tucked, rather than yanking the weight up. Bring your chest up as the weight comes up to retract and get a significant contraction. Keeping your chest up and chin tucked at all times helps avoid strain in your lower back.
* **Arnold Press** - [00:06:17](https://youtu.be/OoA7a2lRxz8?t=377)
    * *Notes:* Unlike a standard military press, the Arnold press involves rotating the weight. Extend fully at the top and rotate the weights back toward you so they are by your eyesight at the bottom.
* **Incline Bench Press (Dumbbell)** - [00:11:12](https://youtu.be/OoA7a2lRxz8?t=672)
    * *Notes:* Keep your shoulders retracted back to isolate the chest. This will naturally bring your chest up and create a slight arch in your back. Drive upwards and use the negative rep to deeply target the chest area. Your legs only serve to push the ground away for stability, not to lift the weight.

## 🧠 General Upper Body Wisdom

* **Combine Push and Pull:** Combining pulling (back/biceps) and pushing (chest/shoulders/triceps) movements in one workout allows for hitting target muscles more frequently (twice a week instead of once) and can feel more efficient ([00:09:43](https://youtu.be/OoA7a2lRxz8?t=583)).
* **Use Structure to Dictate Weight:** A structured program gives you rep targets. If an exercise calls for 6 reps and you can easily do 10, the weight is too light. Your working weight should make the target rep range feel appropriately challenging ([00:03:29](https://youtu.be/OoA7a2lRxz8?t=209)).
* **The "Chest Up" Rule for Pulling:** A key tip for the entire workout: whenever you are rowing or pulling, bring your chest up. This completely isolates the back muscles and allows for a deeper, stronger contraction while protecting the lower back ([00:16:03](https://youtu.be/OoA7a2lRxz8?t=963)).
* **Push Through the Final Set:** When you get to the last set and feel exhausted, dig deep. This is your personal time to prove to yourself that you can do it—fight for that last rep because it counts the most ([00:13:05](https://youtu.be/OoA7a2lRxz8?t=785)).
