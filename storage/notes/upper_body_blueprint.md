### Part 1: Heavy Compound Movements
*Do these individually. 4 Sets each, 8-10 Reps.*

* **Push Press** - [00:02:24](https://youtu.be/wWEKiu5ok6o?t=144)
    * *Notes:* Unlike a strict military press, use your legs to power the weight up. Keep your core tight. It's an explosive, power-driven movement.
* **Inverted Bent Over Row (Reverse Grip)** - [00:03:29](https://youtu.be/wWEKiu5ok6o?t=209)
    * *Notes:* Feet slightly wider than shoulder-width, knees slightly bent to remove lower back pressure. Hold the bar with a reverse (underhand) grip. Pull with your elbows toward your belly button. Retract your shoulders back and keep your chin tucked. 
* **Incline Chest Press (Barbell or Dumbbell)** - [00:05:39](https://youtu.be/wWEKiu5ok6o?t=339)
    * *Notes:* Plant your feet firmly, retract your shoulders back. If you hit a "sticky point," you can push your hips up slightly to help drive the weight.

### Part 2: Superset 1
*Perform these two exercises back-to-back with minimal rest.*

* **Wide Grip Lat Pulldown** - [00:08:34](https://youtu.be/wWEKiu5ok6o?t=514)
    * *Notes:* Keep hands balanced on the bar. Use a thumbless grip (thumbs over the bar) to prevent forearm takeover. Pull down with your elbows and allow your torso to naturally rock back and forward slightly to fully contract the lats.
* **Reverse Grip Front Cable Raise** - [00:10:00](https://youtu.be/wWEKiu5ok6o?t=600)
    * *Notes:* Bend knees slightly and lean forward a bit to isolate the front delts and upper chest. Hold for two seconds at the top of the movement before coming down.

### Part 3: Superset 2
*Perform these two exercises back-to-back with minimal rest.*

* **Single Arm Row** - [00:11:41](https://youtu.be/wWEKiu5ok6o?t=701)
    * *Notes:* Same principles as the barbell row. Keep legs stationary to avoid bouncing, keep your core tight, and pull with the elbow. You can push your non-working hand against a wall or bench for maximum stability.
* **Lateral Raise** - [00:13:19](https://youtu.be/wWEKiu5ok6o?t=799)
    * *Notes:* Slight bend in the knees and elbows, chest upright. Lean slightly forward to isolate the delts better. Avoid swinging with your legs.
